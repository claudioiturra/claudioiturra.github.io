BASIC CHARACTERISTICS OF NEAR-INERTIAL OSCILLATIONS

  • Vertical scales of 100–300 m and tend to rotate clockwise with depth (Leaman & Sanford, 1975).
  • High intermittency or strong time variability (Fu 1981).
  • Strong shear (Doherty et al. 1999).
  • Major contributor to upper-ocean mixing (Jochum et al. 2012).
  • Because of the resonance at f f f in the equations of motion, nearly any forcing will excite near- inertial motions.

Wind to near-inertial motions
  • Wind work on ocean inertial motion.
  • Wind stress from reanalysis and ocean surface near-inertial motion from slab model (Pollard & Millard, 1970)
  • Enhanced near-inertial wind work during storms.
  • Total work done of the same order as tidal dissipation by conversion to internal tides.

Decay mechanisms for mixed layer near-inertial currents
  • Radiation of downward-propagation NIO's. Gill (1984), D'Asaro (1989), Young & Ben Jelloul (1997).
  • Nonlinear interactions transferring energy to other frequencies. Henyey et al.(1896)
  • Turbulent Dissipation. Hebert & Moum (1993)
  • High-frequency internal waves. Polton et al. (2008)

Relative importance of each mechanism is unclear.

Study Area

  • Continental shelf of Concepcion, Chile.

17

Study Area

  • The coastal area around Concepción is an important seasonal upwelling zone (EBUS) and sustains a high fishery production during the year.
  • Strong subtidal signal.
  • Weak tidal circulation.
  • Strong near inertial current (Sobarzo et al. 2007).

18

NIO's around the continental shelf of Concepción.


  • Near inertial energy accounts for up to 60% of the total observed current variance.
  • Amplitudes of the near-inertial currents were stronger near the shelf break (about 20 cm s−1) and weaker near the coast (about 5 cm s−1).
  • Subinertial background flow changes the effective Coriolis frequency, generating resonance between the near-inertial currents and the daily wind cycle.
  • Intermittency in the enhanced near-inertial motions varied around 3.5–10 days and appears to be related to the beat period between diurnal and near-inertial frequencies.

Goal



  • The goal is to describe near-inertial oscillations over the continental shelf of Concepcion, Chile.

Hypotheses and objectives
  • Hypotheses:

The following hypotheses will be evaluated over the Shallow shelf of Concepcion, Chile:

  1. During summer season, NIO’s are generated by the diurnal wind cycle which intensifies regularly over time due the resonance between diurnal and near inertial frequencies.
  2. During winter season, NIO’s are generated after the passing of storms without presenting the regular intensification and intermittency typical of summer season.
  3. Especially during summer, due the higher thermal stratification of the water column. NIO’s generate vertical fluctuations of the isotherms.
22
  • Specific objectives:
  1. Describe the temporal and spatial distribution of NIO’s putting emphasis on the mixed layer or upper ocean.
  2. Evaluate the potential of the diurnal cycle of the wind in the generation of NIO’s during periods of wind favorable to upwelling and relaxation. Evaluate the role of north windstorms in the generation of NIO’s.
  3. Investigate if NIO’s generate the fluctuations of the isotherms in the water column.
  4. Describe the study area using satellite data (SST, Chlorophyll, altimetry, etc.), with the purpose to evaluate the effect on NIO’s in the upper/surface ocean.

Specific objective I

  • The spatial and temporal distribution of NIO’s over the continental shelf of Concepcion will be investigated using current data from ADCP (1h resolution) and Hycom (3h resolution).
  • The current velocity will be filtered to remove the tidal energy and focus only on the near-inertial energy band (0.8f-1.2f).
  • The temporal variability in the current velocity data will be evaluated with spectral and wavelet analysis. The frequencies will be analyzed statistically, in order to evaluate the variability of their sub-inertial and super-inertial components.
  • Rotary spectra of the current velocity (Gonella, 1972) will be used to extract and evaluate the clockwise and counter-clockwise motions.
  • The anisotropy of the current velocity will be evaluated using the Cartesian spectrum. Because NIO’s consist of nearly circular ellipses, their variability is strongly projected onto the Cartesian components.
26
  • The inertial current magnitude will be calculated with 3 indexes:
  1. kinetic Index (KI)
  2. Inertial Radius Index (Ir)
  3. Near inertial Max Spectra Index (NS)

  • The decay scale of the near-inertial energy associated with the current will be calculated using the method described by Xiang et al. 2017.
27
  • Semi-implicit models (Euler's method to solve the equations of horizontal motion) will be used to represent and predict the characteristic ellipse of the NIO’s (Lilly et al, 2018).

Expected results


  • During the summer season and for the upper ocean, NIO's are generated by the diurnal wind cycle and the intensity of the inertial current increase during wind relaxation events. The transfer of inertial energy from the winds to the upper ocean will be intense when the effective Coriolis period is close to 24 h and the mixed layer is shallow. The effective Coriolis frequency is strongly influenced by large-scale ocean dynamics and low-frequency wind. During periods of weak tidal current (absence of tidal mixing), NIO's will generate isothermal oscillations, increasing the vertical mixing through the pycnocline.

  • During the winter season, the passage of storms across the study region (or close to the study region) will transfer inertial energy to the upper ocean. The shallow mixed layer influenced by the freshwater fluxes from the Itata and Bio-Bio river will optimize the intensity of NIO's in the mixed layer. Intense NIO's in the mixed layer will propagate downward generating critical zones with intense isothermal fluctuations. The damped slab model shows that during storms event the local wind induced near-inertial oscillation (NIO) in the mixed layer.
31

Paper 01: The first manuscript will describe the inertial and sub-inertial circulation during the summer, fall, and winter seasons. The transfer of near-inertial energy from the winds to the upper ocean will be evaluated with the damped slab model. The rotary component of storms during winter and the resonance between diurnal and inertial frequencies during summer will be correlated with Nio’s. Changes in the effective Coriolis frequency generated by the variation of the local vorticity and other processes (eddies) that affect the intensity of NIO's in the upper ocean will be also evaluated.

Paper 02: The second manuscript will describe the consequences in terms of temperature and mixing of the temporal and spatial variation of near-inertial oscillations (propagation and dissipation) in the water column. How internal waves respond to the isothermal fluctuation associated with the high intermittency of NIO's, and How the background vorticity significantly modulates NIO’s variability in the thermocline will be evaluated. Surface parameters like SST and Chlorophyll will be correlated with the variation of near-inertial energy in the upper ocean.
